Fixture design checklist.

Use this before you release a new fixture, nest, or workholding concept. It is meant to catch the common issues that look small in review but become painful on the floor.

Discuss the weak points
Part handling

Loading and seating

  • Can the operator load the part without fighting visibility or hand clearance?
  • Is the correct orientation obvious before the part reaches the critical process?
  • Does the part seat positively, or can it appear correct while still being mislocated?
  • Are datum and locating features protected from wear or accidental damage?
Operator use

Cycle and ergonomics

  • Is the clamp sequence intuitive, or does it invite missed steps?
  • Can changeover happen quickly without hidden adjustments or shims?
  • Are inspection points accessible without awkward repositioning?
  • Does the reset state make it obvious the fixture is ready for the next cycle?
Variation and release

Tolerance and build readiness

  • Have tolerance stack-up and part variation been considered under real station conditions?
  • Will wear, contamination, or repeated loading change how the fixture behaves?
  • Is there any poka-yoke feature preventing the most likely operator mistake?
  • Are drawings and release details clear enough for fabrication without verbal interpretation?

If multiple boxes on this checklist feel weak, the fixture probably needs engineering attention.

Mark up the risky areas, then bring them into a short diagnostic. That is usually enough to see whether the issue is geometry, workflow, operator interaction, or all three.
