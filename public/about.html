About EngineeringXYZ

A focused practice for machine prototypes, fixtures, and production equipment.

EngineeringXYZ is led by Dr. Marco Wu and built around practical machine work: custom machine prototypes, fixture design, engineering drawings, production hardware, and line-level problem solving for manufacturing teams that need execution, not noise.

Machine prototypes Production stations Engineering drawings Inspection tooling
What clients hire us for

Focused engineering support that can move from concept to release.

The goal is to help teams solve physical production problems with clean machine thinking, robust hardware architecture, and the kind of drawings and documentation that reduce confusion once the build starts.

  • Machine prototype concepts and mechanism development.
  • Fixture and tooling design for repeatable handling and inspection.
  • Assembly drawings, detail drawings, and release packages.
  • Machine architecture shaped around guarding, access, and uptime.
  • Automation cell improvements tied to throughput and reliability.
Lead engineer

Dr. Marco Wu

A Ph.D. mechanical engineer with more than a decade of experience delivering automation systems, production equipment, and patented manufacturing concepts.

Working style

Manufacturing constraints come first.

Every project is shaped around cycle time, service access, tolerance stack-up, operator use, and what the team can realistically build, revise, and maintain.

Approach

Designed for the realities after the concept has to become a working station.

Strong machine work is not just concepting. It has to survive fabrication feedback, assembly access, operator habits, guarding constraints, and the startup issues that show up on live production equipment.

Build-ready output

Drawings that reduce handoff friction

Frames, mechanisms, tooling details, assembly drawings, and station information are organized so the build team can move cleanly.

Machine architecture

Sequenced around real production flow

Part presentation, transfer, guarding, process timing, and service access are treated as one station problem.

Floor feedback

Improvement does not stop at release

Design decisions stay connected to startup issues, operator experience, and production bottlenecks.
